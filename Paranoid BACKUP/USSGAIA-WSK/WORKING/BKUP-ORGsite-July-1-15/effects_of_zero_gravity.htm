The effects of zero gravity


Biological Effects of Zero Gravity:

  • Muscle mass loss
  • Bone mass loss
  • Brain stem cell loss
  • Immune system reduction
After one year of constant exposure to zero gravity the effects will be permanent. If our species wishes to live in space longer than one year we will have to build habitats that have artificial gravity. The only known way to create artificial gravity is to use centripetal force, the force produced by spinning. The two best three dimensional shapes for spinning habitats are the cylinder and the torus.

Depending on the spin rate the structure will create a variable artificial gravity. A variable gravity is also achieved within the cylinder. If the inside surface spins to achieve one gravity, then the gravity will decrease as you move toward the center of the radius of the cylinder.

Creating Variable Gravity Habitats

The creation of variable gravity habitats will be a game changing event in the pursuit of sustainable living in space. Transporting material from earth to earth's orbit comes with a very high price. If we are going to live in space and expand we will need building material to build our structures in space. That will mean finding materials that are already in space and are less expensive to retrieve then bringing them up earth's gravity well. The first habitat will need to be created with earth based materials but the size and shape of the first habitat will have the capability to use the facility to mine Near Earth Objects (NEO). The first habitat will provide a human safe environment that can extract the valuable elements from the NEO, process them and get them to the construction site for use in building another new habitat.
